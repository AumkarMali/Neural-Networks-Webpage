What are Neural Networks?

Neural networks, alternatively known as simulated neural networks (SNNs) or artificial neural networks (ANNs) are a type of machine learning that takes inspiration from how our brain's neurons interact to enable us to learn and process information. These networks form the basis upon which deep learning algorithms are built.
There are three common types of neural networks: feedforward neural networks, convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). Each have its own specific benefits and optimal use cases. To begin, feedforward neural network are often used as the base for natural language processing, computer vision and even other neural networks. It can be abstracted into three different layers, consisting of the input, the hidden layer(s) and the final output. How these layers faciliate the "learning" for a feedforward neural network is explored in depth here. Next, a convolutional neural network adds on to the feedforward one by borrowing concepts, i.e. the multiplication of matrices, from linear algebra. It is used in pattern and image recognition, as well as computer vision. Lastly, recurrent neural networks are trained through feedback loops. These are popular when making predictions based on past data over a time period, like for stocks.
